About Vermicompost

Vermicompost is the product of the decomposition process using various species of worms, usually red wigglers, white worms, and other earthworms, to create a mixture of decomposing vegetable or food waste, bedding materials, and vermicast.


Benefits

  • Improves soil aeration and texture.
  • Enriches soil with micro-organisms (adding enzymes such as phosphatase and cellulase).
  • Microbial activity in worm castings is 10 to 20 times higher than in the soil and organic matter that the worm consumes.
  • Attracts deep-burrowing earthworms already present in the soil.
  • Enhances water holding capacity.

Preparation Method

To prepare vermicompost, organic waste is placed in layers in a pit or tank. Earthworms are introduced to the waste. The pit is kept moist and covered. Over a period of 45-60 days, the worms consume the waste and excrete nutrient-rich castings.
